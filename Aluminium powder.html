More Details About This Product

Production: Aluminium powder is typically produced through processes such as atomization, grinding, or milling of aluminium ingots or sheets. These processes create fine particles of aluminium that can range in size from micrometers to nanometers.
Properties: Lightweight: Aluminium powder is lightweight, making it suitable for applications where weight reduction is important, such as in aerospace components and lightweight materials.
High Thermal Conductivity: Aluminium has excellent thermal conductivity, making aluminium powder useful in applications such as thermal paste for electronic components and heat transfer materials.
Reactivity: Due to its high reactivity with oxygen, aluminium powder is used in pyrotechnics, explosives, and flares. It reacts vigorously with oxygen to produce heat and light.
Flammability: Fine aluminium powder is highly flammable and can ignite easily, making it a key ingredient in fireworks, sparklers, and metal powders used for special effects.
Applications: Pyrotechnics: Aluminium powder is a key component in fireworks, sparklers, flares, and other pyrotechnic devices due to its ability to create bright flashes of light and intense heat when ignited.
Metallurgy: Aluminium powder is used in metallurgical processes, such as powder metallurgy, to produce metal parts, components, and alloys with specific properties.
Thermal Paste: Aluminium powder mixed with thermal grease or thermal compound is used as thermal paste for electronic devices to improve heat transfer and cooling efficiency.
Explosives and Propellants: Aluminium powder is used in the production of explosives, propellants, and rocket fuels due to its high energy release upon combustion.
Safety Considerations: Fine aluminium powder can be hazardous if mishandled, as it is flammable and can create explosive mixtures when dispersed in air. Proper safety precautions, handling procedures, and storage practices are essential when working with aluminium powder.

More Details About This Product

Composition: Aluminium ingots are primarily composed of aluminium, often with small percentages of other elements added to enhance specific properties.
For example, alloying elements like copper, silicon, magnesium, zinc, and manganese are commonly added to aluminium to create aluminium alloys with desirable characteristics such as increased strength, corrosion resistance, or heat resistance.
Manufacturing Process: Aluminium ingots are typically produced through a process called smelting. In this process, alumina (aluminium oxide) is refined from bauxite ore through the Bayer process or the Hall-Héroult process.
The alumina is then electrolyzed in a smelting furnace, where it is converted into molten aluminium. The molten aluminium is then poured into molds to solidify and form ingots.
Types of Aluminium Ingots:
Pure Aluminium Ingots: These ingots are made of nearly pure aluminium, typically with a purity of 99.7% or higher. They are used in applications where high conductivity or corrosion resistance is required, such as electrical components, packaging materials, and food containers.
Aluminium Alloy Ingots: These ingots are made by alloying aluminium with other elements to create alloys with specific properties. Common aluminium alloys include 1000 series (pure aluminium), 2000 series (copper alloys), 3000 series (manganese alloys), 5000 series (magnesium alloys), and 6000 series (silicon and magnesium alloys). Aluminium alloy ingots are used in a wide range of industries, including automotive, aerospace, construction, and manufacturing.
Applications: Aluminium ingots and alloys have numerous applications due to their lightweight, corrosion resistance, electrical conductivity, and thermal conductivity. Some common applications include: Automotive parts and components (e.g., engine blocks, wheels, body panels) Aerospace structures and aircraft components (e.g., fuselage, wings, landing gear) Building and construction materials (e.g., window frames, roofing, structural beams) Electrical conductors and wiring Packaging materials (e.g., cans, foils, containers) Industrial machinery and equipment Consumer goods and appliances Market and Trade: Aluminium ingots are traded globally as a commodity. Prices can fluctuate based on factors such as supply and demand, global economic conditions, currency exchange rates, and trade policies.
